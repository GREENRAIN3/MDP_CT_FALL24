Flocking Simulation

Design Explanation

The Flocking Simulation is a visual representation of collective movement behaviors seen in nature, such as birds flying in formation or fish swimming in schools. The algorithm is inspired by Craig Reynolds' Boids model, which simulates three basic behaviors: alignment, cohesion, and separation. In this project, we further explore these principles by adding noise-based randomization to the movement, making the patterns more organic and dynamic.

Users can toggle between Perlin Noise-based movement and the traditional flocking behavior by interacting with the canvas. The simulation demonstrates how simple rules can lead to complex and visually captivating group behaviors, reinforcing concepts in emergent systems and computational design.
