Fine-tuning different aspects in TouchDesigner, focusing on transformations and camera movement to achieve a more detailed growing visual. This stage is critical to bring subtle changes to life through fluid motion.

Camera angles and movement adjustments in TouchDesigner were key here. By experimenting with depth and motion, I wanted to replicate the organic expansion of intricate structures.

Procedural animation was introduced to simulate dynamic and evolving patterns. Feedback loops amplified the sense of continuous growth, bringing the visuals to life.

Applying shader effects added another layer of complexity. The variations in color and noise create visuals that feel fluid and organic, always shifting and expanding.

The final step in After Effects, where all the visual elements created in TouchDesigner were brought together. By blending layers and enhancing depth, I achieved a more immersive and polished growing effect.

This project draws inspiration from Yoshi Sodeoka's rich generative art style. The theme of "growing" was explored through TouchDesigner, where I used a combination of transformations, camera adjustments, and procedural techniques to create visuals that feel alive and continuously expanding.

Each process builds upon the last. I began with experimenting with dynamic camera work and transformation effects, simulating natural growth patterns. As I progressed, I introduced procedural animations and shader effects, adding vibrancy and fluidity to the visuals. Finally, all elements were combined in After Effects, where the layering techniques brought a sense of depth and cohesion to the overall composition.

The goal of this project was to create an experience that captures the feeling of organic growth, where visuals unfold and evolve, much like nature itself.
